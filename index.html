About the accreditation

After putting in a lot of effort preparing for an interview, spending money on new clothes and paying for travel expenses, it can be disappointing to not get any feedback from the employer in question post-interview.


Research undertaken by Debut found that 77% of people aged 18-23 would like to see feedback after a face-to-face interview made obligatory. The Fight for Feedback campaign was launched in the interest of candidates who are trying to secure employment and has since attracted the support of human resource stakeholders. So far, the campaign has received extensive media coverage and has already attracted over 1,000 supporters, including major employers and industry stakeholders.

Our feedback reccomendations



1
Manage Expectations

Manage all candidates’ expectations by clearly explaining your recruitment and selection procedure on your company’s website.

2 Provide Feedback

Proactively share feedback after a face-to-face interview, in place of only offering feedback if the candidate requests it.

3 Type of Feedback

Share objective feedback about the candidates’ competencies in relation to the job role / person specification as a minimum.

4 Feedback Medium

Establish how the candidate would prefer to receive feedback – this could be stipulated during the application process.

5 Constructive & Positive

Deliver feedback in a positive way by identifying what a candidate did well, and ways in which they can be more successful next time.

6 Person Responsible

Avoid watering down feedback by involving too many parties in the feedback process.

7 Time Investment

Aim to feed back within three working days after the hiring decision has been made.
